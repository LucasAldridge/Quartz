DEMAND
Demand is the curve showing the amount people want to buy a thing at ALL prices. QUANTITY DEMANDED is a specific point on that curve showing how many items are needed to fulfill the demand at that price.

there is a NEGATIVE relationship between price and QUANTITY DEMANDED. as price goes up, demand goes down, as price goes down, demand goes up. because of this, the demand curve is ALWAYS downward sloping. 

WHY?
substitution - when shit gets expensive, you find a heuristic instead of paying a lot of money
real balances effect - when prices go up, it decreases the consumers real buying power, so they spend less
diminishing marginal utility - the amount of gain you feel from buying a product decreases the more of that product you have.
