## Type 1 -
- an infinite bound(s)
- infinity is not a number that you can plug into a jawn
- if seen, write limit as T approaches infinity from a to t f(x)
EX - 
int from 1 to infinity 1/x^2 dx, or lim as t approaches t, integral from 1 to t 1/x^2 dx ->
lim as t approaches infity, -1/x from 1 to t, lim as t approaches infinity from -1/t +1. if t is infinitely large, the answer is 1. 
in a scenario like this, provided that the limit exists, we say that the integral from A to infinity, we say it CONVERGES
in a situation where the limit DNE, we say it DIVERGES

integral between -inf and inf. Use integration by parts and add them together, replacing the top and bottom bounds w/ zero, and replace the other bound like we did w/ type 1. Both have to exist for the integral to converge. Otherwise, it is divergent
